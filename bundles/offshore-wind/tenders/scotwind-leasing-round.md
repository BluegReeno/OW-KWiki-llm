# Overview

ScotWind is a Scottish seabed **leasing round**, not a price/subsidy
auction like the UK CfD scheme
([UK Contracts for Difference scheme](/policy/uk-contracts-for-difference-scheme.md)) —
it grants Option Agreements for the right to develop specific seabed
sites, in exchange for an upfront option fee to Crown Estate Scotland;
projects still need to separately secure planning consent and (typically)
compete in a later CfD round to sell their power. Results were announced
by Crown Estate Scotland on **17 January 2022** (with a Scottish
Government ministerial statement the following day), selecting 17 winning
projects from 74 applications submitted by developers and consortia.

Because water is deep across much of Scotland's coast, more than half the
awarded capacity — 10 of the 17 projects — uses **floating** wind
technology (6 are fixed-bottom, 1 uses both). The largest single award was
ScottishPower Renewables' (Iberdrola) 3 GW floating MarramWind project, a
joint bid with Shell; BP and EnBW's 2.9 GW fixed-bottom Morven was the
largest fixed-bottom award. Total option fees paid to the Scottish
Government across all winners came to almost £700 million.

As of mid-2026, ScotWind has seen some attrition and restructuring from
its original award list: Shell has exited its ScotWind joint venture with
ScottishPower, and the 3 GW CampionWind lease was handed back to Crown
Estate Scotland. Individual project capacities below should be treated as
the originally-awarded figures — several have since been revised through
consenting and design changes.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Organizer | Crown Estate Scotland |
| Process type | Seabed leasing round (Option Agreements) — not a CfD/subsidy auction |
| Applications received | 74 |
| Results announced | 17 January 2022 |
| Total capacity awarded | ~24,826 MW (~24.8 GW) across 17 projects, 11 developers/consortia |
| Technology mix | 10 floating, 6 fixed-bottom, 1 mixed |
| Total option fees | ~£700 million |
| Largest floating award | MarramWind, ~3 GW — ScottishPower Renewables (Iberdrola) / Shell (Shell has since exited the JV) |
| Largest fixed-bottom award | Morven, ~2.9 GW — BP / EnBW |
| Other notable awards | CampionWind (~2 GW floating, ScottishPower/Shell — lease since handed back); Stromar (~1 GW floating, Ørsted/Falck Renewables/BlueFloat Energy); Talisk (~495 MW floating, Magnora) |
| Status (mid-2026) | Projects at varying consenting/development stages; some restructuring among original winners (e.g. Shell exit) |
