# Overview

AO1 (appel d'offres n°1) was France's first offshore wind call for
tenders, launched by the French State on 11 July 2011 for up to 3,000 MW
of fixed-bottom capacity spread across five zones along the Channel and
Atlantic coasts: Le Tréport, Fécamp, Courseulles-sur-Mer, Saint-Brieuc,
and Saint-Nazaire. Bids were due in January 2012 and the winners were
announced on 6 April 2012 after review by the CRE (Commission de
Régulation de l'Énergie).

Four of the five zones were awarded. The consortium Éolien Maritime France
(EMF) — led by EDF Énergies Nouvelles (now EDF Renouvelables) with Danish
partner Dong Energy Power, using Alstom turbines — won Fécamp (498 MW),
Courseulles-sur-Mer (450 MW), and Saint-Nazaire (480 MW). Ailes Marines SAS
— then led by [Iberdrola](/companies/iberdrola.md) with EOLE-RES, using
Areva turbines — won Saint-Brieuc (500 MW). The fifth zone, Le Tréport, was
declared "sans suite" (without follow-up): its bid carried the highest
electricity purchase price of the five zones, and was rejected to avoid an
estimated €500 million in additional cost. Le Tréport was subsequently
re-tendered in the second round (AO2, 2014) and awarded, as the
[Dieppe–Le Tréport](/projects/dieppe-le-treport.md) project, to an
Engie-led consortium; all jacket foundations were installed by
[DEME Offshore](/companies/deme-offshore.md) in June 2026.

The four AO1 awards totalled roughly 1,928 MW — close to 2,000 MW of the
3,000 MW offered — and became France's first offshore wind farms:
[Saint-Nazaire](/projects/saint-nazaire.md), commissioned in November 2022
as the country's first operating offshore wind farm, followed by
[Saint-Brieuc](/projects/saint-brieuc.md) (2024), Fécamp (2024), and
Courseulles-sur-Mer. The AO1 feed-in tariffs, set at roughly €200/MWh,
were high by later standards; the French State renegotiated them downward
by about 30% (to roughly €150/MWh) in an agreement announced on
20 June 2018, covering the six projects from the 2012 and 2014 tenders.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Organizer | French State (Ministère de l'Écologie), with review by the CRE (Commission de Régulation de l'Énergie) |
| Market | France — Channel and Atlantic coasts (Seine-Maritime, Calvados, Côtes-d'Armor, Loire-Atlantique) |
| Official designation | AO1 (1st French offshore wind tender) |
| Launched | 11 July 2011 |
| Winners announced | 6 April 2012 |
| Capacity offered | Up to 3,000 MW across five zones |
| Capacity awarded | ~1,928 MW across four zones |
| Zones & winners | Fécamp (498 MW) — Éolien Maritime France; Courseulles-sur-Mer (450 MW) — Éolien Maritime France; Saint-Nazaire (480 MW) — Éolien Maritime France; Saint-Brieuc (500 MW) — Ailes Marines SAS; Le Tréport — declared without follow-up |
| Consortiums | Éolien Maritime France: EDF Énergies Nouvelles + Dong Energy Power, Alstom turbines. Ailes Marines: Iberdrola + EOLE-RES, Areva turbines |
| Feed-in tariff | ~€200/MWh at award; renegotiated ~30% lower (~€150/MWh) in June 2018 |
| Status (mid-2026) | All four awarded farms commissioned (Saint-Nazaire 2022, Saint-Brieuc & Fécamp 2024, Courseulles-sur-Mer) |
