# Overview

Centre Manche 1 is a French fixed-bottom offshore wind tender for a site
off the coast of Normandy (Baie de Seine), run by the CRE on behalf of the
French State. It was awarded in March 2023 to Éoliennes en Mer Manche
Normandie (EMMN), a joint venture between EDF Renouvelables and the
Canadian developer Maple Power. It is the first of two "Centre Manche"
tenders in the same zone — Centre Manche 2 (a separate, later tender) was
awarded in 2024/2025 to Cotentin Energies Marines, a joint venture between
TotalEnergies and RWE, at €66/MWh for 1,500 MW; together the two projects
form a combined ~2.5 GW Centre Manche cluster.

**Note on numbering:** Centre Manche 1's official CRE designation is **AO4**
(4th French offshore wind tender). It should not be confused with AO6, a
separate, unrelated tender: two 250 MW-class floating wind projects in the
Mediterranean (Narbonnaise Sud-Hérault and Golfe de Fos), awarded in
December 2024 to Ocean Winds and EDF Renouvelables/Maple Power
respectively.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Organizer | CRE (Commission de Régulation de l'Énergie), on behalf of the French State |
| Market | France — Manche Mer du Nord maritime facade, off Normandy (Baie de Seine) |
| Official designation | AO4 (4th French offshore wind tender), launched December 2020, competitive dialogue from January 2021 |
| Capacity | ~1,000 MW, fixed-bottom |
| Bid deadline | November 2022 |
| Winner announced | 27 March 2023 |
| Winner | Éoliennes en Mer Manche Normandie (EMMN) — joint venture of EDF Renouvelables and Maple Power (Canada) |
| Winning price | €44.90/MWh (excluding indexation) |
| Status (mid-2026) | In development/environmental and technical studies; construction targeted ~2028; commercial operation targeted ~2032 |
