# Overview

"Sud Atlantique" — the offshore wind zone near the Île d'Oléron
(Charente-Maritime) — has an unusually troubled tender history among
French offshore wind rounds, and does not have a single clean "AO9"
answer. It is documented here in full rather than compressed into one
number, since getting this wrong would misrepresent the actual state of
the market.

1. **AO7 ("Oléron 1"), ~1 GW** — the original standalone Sud Atlantique
   tender, launched following public debate (CNDP) consultations in
   2021–2022. Nine candidates were pre-qualified by the CRE, but **none
   submitted a bid** by the deadline; the CRE/government declared the
   tender unsuccessful ("infructueux") on **24 September 2025**.
   Industry cited the site's deep waters (60–70 m), a less favorable wind
   regime than other French zones, distance from shore, and a guaranteed
   price considered too low to be economically viable.
2. **AO9** — a competitive dialogue launched in July 2024 covering four
   projects (an extension of the Bretagne Sud floating farm awarded under
   AO5, two Mediterranean floating extensions related to AO6, and one
   Sud Atlantique component). AO9 did not conclude as a standalone round:
   it was merged into AO10 (announced ~April 2026) to streamline
   procurement and reduce transaction costs.
3. **AO10** — the resulting mega-tender, covering 11 projects across all
   French maritime facades (~10 GW total, split roughly 5 GW fixed-bottom
   / 5 GW floating), including one fixed-bottom and one floating project
   at Oléron (~1.2 GW combined, up from AO7's original 1 GW). Candidates
   have four months to submit offers to the CRE; a CRE opinion is
   expected in early 2027, with ministerial attribution targeted for
   **February 2027**.

**No winner has been announced for Sud Atlantique / Oléron offshore wind
as of July 2026.** Any claim of a named winner or a concluded "AO9 Sud
Atlantique" tender would be incorrect at this time.
