# Overview

UK Contracts for Difference (CfD) Allocation Round 7 (AR7) is administered
under the mechanism described in
[UK Contracts for Difference scheme](/policy/uk-contracts-for-difference-scheme.md) —
this page covers only what is specific to this round. Unlike prior rounds,
AR7 was split into two parallel procedures: **AR7** covered fixed-bottom
and floating offshore wind exclusively, while **AR7a** covered other
technologies (onshore wind, solar, tidal) with results in
February 2026. AR7 also introduced 20-year CPI-indexed contracts (up from
15 years) and relaxed eligibility to let offshore wind projects apply
while still awaiting full planning consent.

Results were announced **14 January 2026**: a record **8.4 GW of offshore
wind capacity** cleared — comfortably beating the previous record of
7.0 GW (AR4) and far above AR6's ~5.3 GW. RWE was the standout winner with
6.9 GW across five projects (Norfolk Vanguard East, Norfolk Vanguard West,
Dogger Bank South East, Dogger Bank South West — the latter two jointly
with Masdar — and Awel y Môr). SSE secured 1.4 GW for Berwick Bank Phase B.
Two floating wind projects also cleared: the 100 MW Erebus (Blue Gem Wind)
in the Celtic Sea and the ~92.5–100 MW Pentland (Copenhagen Infrastructure
Partners) in Scotland.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Organizer | DESNZ (Department for Energy Security and Net Zero) sets budget/parameters; NESO (National Energy System Operator), as Electricity Market Reform Delivery Body, administers the auction |
| Market | United Kingdom — AR7 covered fixed-bottom and floating offshore wind only (other technologies ran separately as AR7a) |
| Application window | Opened 7 August 2025 |
| Results announced | 14 January 2026 (offshore wind); AR7a non-offshore results 6–9 February 2026 |
| Capacity awarded | ~8.4–8.5 GW total: ~6,865 MW offshore wind (rest of GB), ~1,380 MW offshore wind (Scotland), ~192.5 MW floating |
| Administrative Strike Price | £113/MWh (2024 prices) for offshore wind |
| Clearing/strike prices | Fixed-bottom cleared around £90–91/MWh (£89.49/MWh for the Scotland pot); floating wind cleared around £216/MWh |
| Budget | £900m (fixed-bottom pot) + £180m (floating pot) |
| Largest winner | RWE — 6.9 GW across Norfolk Vanguard East/West, Dogger Bank South East/South West (with Masdar), and Awel y Môr |
| Other winners | SSE (Berwick Bank Phase B, 1.4 GW); Blue Gem Wind (Erebus, floating, 100 MW); Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (Pentland, floating, ~92.5–100 MW) |
| Status | Concluded — contracts awarded |
