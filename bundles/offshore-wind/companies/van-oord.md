# Role

Royal Van Oord is a Dutch marine contractor headquartered in Rotterdam, the
Netherlands, founded in 1868 by Govert van Oord. It remains majority family
owned — the Van Oord family holding company MerweOord B.V. holds roughly
78.5% of shares — and is one of the world's largest dredging and marine
engineering firms. Its offshore wind division installs monopile and jacket
foundations and inter-array/export cables for wind farms, acting as a
contractor for developers such as [Ørsted](/companies/orsted.md),
[RWE](/companies/rwe-offshore-wind.md), and [Iberdrola](/companies/iberdrola.md)
rather than developing projects itself. Reported annual revenue is in the
$3–5 billion range depending on the source and year.

# Fleet / Capabilities

- **Aeolus** — jack-up installation vessel used for foundation installation
  campaigns (e.g. Saint-Brieuc).
- **Calypso** and **Nexus** — dedicated cable-laying vessels, Calypso a newer
  hybrid vessel, Nexus fitted with a 5,000-tonne cable carousel.
- **Dig-It** — Van Oord's own subsea trencher for cable burial.
- **Vox Apolonia** — LNG-powered hopper dredger, also used for cable-trench
  pre-excavation work.
- Van Oord has also ordered a new mega installation vessel designed to handle
  20 MW-class turbine foundations and components.

# Key Projects

- [Saint-Brieuc](/projects/saint-brieuc.md) — installed the first of 62
  jacket foundations in May 2021 using the vessel Aeolus, for
  [Iberdrola](/companies/iberdrola.md)'s Bay of Saint-Brieuc wind farm.
- [Changhua](/projects/changhua.md) — cable installation contractor for
  [Ørsted](/companies/orsted.md)'s Greater Changhua offshore wind farms in
  Taiwan: transport, installation, and burial of 111 inter-array cables and
  3 export cables (~135 km and ~145 km respectively) at Greater Changhua
  1 & 2a, and export cable installation at Greater Changhua 2b & 4, using the
  vessel Nexus and trencher Dig-It.

Other recent installation contracts (not yet covered as bundle pages)
include Hollandse Kust West VI and Sofia (Netherlands/UK, monopile and cable
installation), Baltic Power (Poland, first offshore wind farm), and
Windanker (Germany, for Iberdrola).
