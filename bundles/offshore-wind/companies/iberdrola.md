# Role

Iberdrola S.A. is a Spanish multinational electric utility and one of the world's largest renewable energy groups, with offshore wind as a core pillar of its growth strategy (around 54% of its 2023-2026 renewables investment plan is committed to offshore wind). In the United States, Iberdrola develops offshore wind through Avangrid, Inc., which became a wholly-owned subsidiary in December 2024 after Iberdrola acquired the remaining 18.4% stake it did not already own (for ~$2.55 billion) and took the company private. In the UK, Iberdrola operates as ScottishPower Renewables.

Iberdrola had around 2.6 GW of installed offshore wind capacity as of early 2026, with a stated ambition to reach roughly 4.8 GW in 2026 and to add 3.5 GW more between 2025 and 2028 (~€8 billion of gross investment).

# Key Projects

- [Vineyard Wind 1](/projects/vineyard-wind-1.md) — Iberdrola/Avangrid's first US offshore wind farm, ~804 MW off Massachusetts, co-developed with Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners; construction was briefly paused in late 2025 by a US federal order before resuming under a court injunction.
- [Saint-Brieuc](/projects/saint-brieuc.md) — 496 MW offshore wind farm in Brittany, France, fully energised; turbines supplied by [Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md).
- East Anglia Hub (UK) — a cluster of projects (East Anglia ONE, ONE North, TWO, THREE) targeting more than 3.9 GW combined; East Anglia THREE (1,400 MW) is Iberdrola's largest wind project globally, under construction with first power expected in 2026. East Anglia TWO secured a Contract for Difference in [UK CfD Allocation Round 6](/tenders/uk-cfd-allocation-round-6.md).
- Baltic Hub (Germany) — Baltic Eagle (~476 MW, operational) and Windanker (~315 MW, commissioning expected late 2026), together with the earlier Wikinger project, forming a >1.1 GW German offshore cluster.
- ScotWind projects (Scotland) — development rights secured in the 2022 [ScotWind leasing round](/tenders/scotwind-leasing-round.md) for MarramWind and CampionWind (floating, with Shell) and MachairWind (fixed-bottom), together targeting around 7 GW.

# Ownership & Structure

Iberdrola S.A. is listed on the Spanish stock exchanges (IBEX 35). Its principal offshore wind vehicles are:
- **Avangrid, Inc.** — US subsidiary, 100% owned since December 2024 (previously ~81.6%, with the remaining 18.4% acquired for ~$2.55 billion and the company taken private).
- **ScottishPower Renewables** — UK brand for Iberdrola's British offshore wind business.
- **Iberdrola Deutschland** — German offshore wind business (Baltic Hub).
