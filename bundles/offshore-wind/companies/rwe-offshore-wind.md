# Role

RWE is a German multinational energy company and one of the world's leading players in offshore wind, describing itself as the number 2 global operator in the sector with over 20 years of experience. It is active across the full value chain — development, construction, and operation — and operates 18-19 offshore wind farms worldwide. RWE has set a target of expanding its global offshore wind capacity to 10 GW by 2030, with more than 4 GW currently under construction in Europe. Core markets are the UK, Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands, alongside a growing but currently strained position in the United States and early-stage development in Taiwan.

In January 2026, RWE was the single biggest winner of the UK's [UK CfD Allocation Round 7](/tenders/uk-cfd-allocation-round-7.md), securing Contracts for Difference for 6.9 GW of offshore wind capacity — Norfolk Vanguard East, Norfolk Vanguard West, Dogger Bank South East, Dogger Bank South West, and Awel y Môr — at a strike price of £91.20/MWh, alongside a long-term partnership with investor KKR on the Norfolk Vanguard projects.

# Key Projects

- **Sofia** (1.4 GW, UK) — RWE's largest single project, located on Dogger Bank in the North Sea, using Siemens Gamesa 14 MW turbines; currently in installation phase with commissioning expected 2026. Note: Sofia is an independently RWE-owned project, distinct from the separate "Dogger Bank Wind Farm" (A/B/C) developed by SSE Renewables and Equinor on a neighbouring site.
- **Thor** (1.1 GW, Denmark) — Denmark's largest offshore wind farm, a joint project of RWE (51%) and Norges Bank Investment Management (49%), using [Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md) turbines with recyclable rotor blades; commissioning expected 2027.
- **Nordseecluster** (1.6 GW, Germany) — developed in two phases (Nordseecluster A: 660 MW, Nordseecluster B: 900 MW), with [Vestas](/companies/vestas.md) selected as preferred turbine supplier; first turbine installed 2026.
- **OranjeWind** (795 MW, Netherlands) — developed with TotalEnergies, which acquired a 50% stake and dedicates its share to green hydrogen production for its European refineries.
- **Windbostel Ost / Windbostel West** (4 GW combined, Germany) — two North Sea sites awarded to RWE in Germany's 2024 offshore auction, being jointly developed with TotalEnergies (50% equity stake); investment decisions expected 2027-2028.
- **Norfolk Vanguard East / West and Dogger Bank South East / West** (UK) — awarded CfDs in AR7 (January 2026); Norfolk Vanguard developed with KKR as strategic partner, FID targeted for summer 2026.
- **Awel y Môr** (UK, Irish Sea) — also awarded a CfD in AR7.
- **Community Offshore Wind** (up to 3 GW potential, New York/New Jersey, US) — lease area OCS-A 0539, developed with National Grid Ventures; paused since April 2025 amid US federal policy disruption, with RWE reportedly in 2026 lease-buyback talks with the US government.
- **Canopy** (1.6 GW potential, California, US) — floating wind lease off Humboldt County.
- **Chu Feng** (up to 448 MW, Taiwan) — early-stage project with Asia Cement Corporation off Hsinchu, part of RWE's push into Asia-Pacific floating and fixed-bottom development.

# Technology Focus

RWE is active in [floating offshore wind](/technology/floating-wind.md) development (Taiwan, US West Coast) alongside its established fixed-bottom portfolio, and pursues offshore [power-to-x](/technology/power-to-x.md) applications, including the RWE-BASF "Offshore-to-X" green hydrogen project and green-hydrogen testbeds at Nordsee One and Kaskasi.

# Recent Developments

- **UK**: Won 6.9 GW in CfD Allocation Round 7 (January 2026), the largest single award in "Europe's biggest ever offshore wind auction."
- **Germany**: Formalised a 4 GW joint development partnership with TotalEnergies for the Windbostel sites (finalised October 2024).
- **United States**: Following a December 2025 US Department of the Interior stop-work order affecting offshore wind construction and broader federal actions disrupting the market, RWE suspended US offshore development activity and was, as of May 2026, reportedly negotiating a lease buyback deal potentially exceeding $1 billion, drawing criticism from environmental and industry groups.
