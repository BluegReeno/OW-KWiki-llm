# Role

GE Vernova is a US energy equipment and services company that was spun off from
General Electric on April 2, 2024 (trading as GEV on the NYSE), taking with it
GE's renewable energy, power, and electrification businesses. Its Wind
business unit designs and manufactures the **Haliade-X**, one of the largest
commercial offshore wind turbine platforms, competing directly with
[Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md) and [Vestas](/companies/vestas.md)
as an offshore turbine OEM.

Since 2024–2025 GE Vernova has significantly scaled back new offshore wind
commitments after sustained heavy losses in the segment — nearly $600 million
in 2025, worse than the $400 million the company had guided, driven partly by
turbine blade incidents at Vineyard Wind and Dogger Bank and by a US federal
stop-work order affecting Vineyard Wind. The company has confirmed it will
service only the turbine orders already in execution (Dogger Bank and
Vineyard Wind 1) and has terminated its remaining supply agreements for
projects not yet under construction, effectively retreating from further
offshore wind expansion.

# Key Turbine Models / Deployments

- **Haliade-X** — launched as a 12 MW prototype in 2019 (220 m rotor diameter),
  the platform has since been uprated to 13 MW and 14 MW production variants;
  GE Vernova has also discussed a 15.5 MW "workhorse" evolution as the
  platform's likely future baseline. An earlier plan to develop an 18 MW
  variant was shelved in 2024.
- **Dogger Bank Wind Farm (UK)** — the largest Haliade-X deployment to date:
  190 Haliade-X 13 MW turbines ordered across Dogger Bank A & B (95 each) and
  87 Haliade-X 14 MW turbines for Dogger Bank C, totaling 277 turbines across
  the 3.6 GW project. See [Dogger Bank](/projects/dogger-bank.md).
- **Vineyard Wind 1 (US)** — 62 Haliade-X 13 MW turbines supplied for the
  first utility-scale offshore wind farm in the United States (806 MW). See
  [Vineyard Wind 1](/projects/vineyard-wind-1.md).

# Key Projects

- [Dogger Bank](/projects/dogger-bank.md) — turbine supplier (Haliade-X 13/14 MW), developed by SSE Renewables, Equinor, and Vårgrønn.
- [Vineyard Wind 1](/projects/vineyard-wind-1.md) — turbine supplier (Haliade-X 13 MW), developed by Avangrid and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners.
