# Role

Equinor is a Norwegian energy company, majority owned by the Norwegian state, historically known as the oil and gas major Statoil (renamed Equinor in 2018). It has built one of the largest offshore wind portfolios among oil and gas majors, combining early leadership in floating offshore wind — it operates [Hywind Scotland](/technology/floating-wind.md), the world's first floating wind farm (commissioned 2017, 30 MW, 5 turbines) — with large-scale fixed-bottom projects in the UK, US, Germany, and Poland.

Equinor has previously stated an ambition to become a "global offshore wind energy major." However, in its 2025 strategy update the company confirmed it will not meet its earlier target of 12–16 GW of installed renewable capacity by 2030, citing rising costs and a thinner project pipeline, and announced roughly USD 4 billion in capex cuts for 2026–2027 concentrated in its power and low-carbon business. Offshore wind remains central to its transition strategy, but Equinor is now prioritizing sanctioning and delivering a smaller number of de-risked, already-financed projects (Dogger Bank, Empire Wind 1, the Bałtyk projects) over pipeline expansion.

# Key Projects

- **[Dogger Bank](/projects/dogger-bank.md)** (UK, North Sea) — the world's largest fixed-bottom offshore wind farm under construction, 3.6 GW across three 1.2 GW phases (A, B, C). Joint venture with SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%) and Vårgrønn (20%). First power delivered in October 2023.
- **[Empire Wind 1](/projects/empire-wind-1.md)** (US, New York Bight) — 810 MW fixed-bottom project off New York, now solely owned by Equinor after a 2024 asset swap with BP (BP took full ownership of Beacon Wind in exchange). Over 60% complete as of late 2025. Construction has been repeatedly disrupted by US federal action: a stop-work order in April 2025 (lifted in May 2025) and a further lease suspension ordered by the Department of the Interior in December 2025, citing national-security concerns; Equinor's Empire Offshore Wind LLC filed suit in January 2026 seeking to overturn the suspension. Target completion has slipped to end of 2026, with commercial operation expected in 2027. A related project, Empire Wind 2, had its power offtake agreement with NYSERDA terminated in early 2024 amid rising costs, and is in a "reset" phase without a confirmed timeline.
- **Hywind Scotland** — world's first floating offshore wind farm, near Peterhead; joint venture of Equinor (75%) and Masdar (25%).
- **Hywind Tampen** — floating wind farm in the Norwegian North Sea (11 turbines) supplying power to Equinor's Snorre and Gullfaks oil and gas platforms to cut their emissions.
- **Bałtyk 2 & 3** (Poland, Baltic Sea) — 1.4 GW under construction with partner Polenergia; first foundations installed in 2026, first power expected 2027. Equinor and Polenergia are also developing **Bałtyk 1** (up to 1,560 MW, targeted commercial operation 2032).
- **Arkona** (Germany, Baltic Sea) — 385 MW operational fixed-bottom farm, 25% Equinor stake.
- **Sheringham Shoal** and **Dudgeon** (UK) — earlier operational fixed-bottom projects.

# Notes on Recent Developments

- ScotWind (Scotland's 2022 seabed leasing round): Equinor bid for floating wind acreage but was not among the 17 winning projects.
- UK Contracts for Difference Allocation Round 6 (2024): Equinor did not secure a new award; Dogger Bank's contracts date from earlier CfD rounds (AR3/AR4), not AR6 or AR7.
- No verified Equinor involvement was found in the Greater Changhua (Taiwan, Ørsted-led), Vineyard Wind 1 (Avangrid/Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners-led), Saint-Brieuc, or Saint-Nazaire projects, so these are not linked here.
