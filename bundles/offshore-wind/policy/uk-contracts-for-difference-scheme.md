# Overview

The **Contracts for Difference (CfD)** scheme is the UK Government's main mechanism for supporting low-carbon electricity generation, including offshore wind. Introduced under the Electricity Market Reform provisions of the **Energy Act 2013**, it works as a two-way financial contract between a generator and the government-owned **Low Carbon Contracts Company (LCCC)**: the generator sells its electricity into the wholesale market, and if the market (reference) price is below the agreed **strike price**, the LCCC pays the generator the difference; if the market price is above the strike price, the generator pays the difference back to the LCCC. This shields both consumers and generators from price volatility while giving developers long-term revenue certainty.

Contracts are allocated through competitive auctions — **Allocation Rounds (AR)** — run by the **National Energy System Operator (NESO)**, formerly the responsibility of National Grid ESO, as Delivery Body, with scheme policy set by the Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ, formerly BEIS/DECC). Technologies compete within separate "pots" grouped by cost/maturity (established technologies, emerging technologies including floating offshore wind, and — since AR4 — offshore wind in its own pot), so that mature and emerging technologies are not bid against each other directly.

The scheme has run annually since 2022 (previously less frequently), and successive allocation rounds have become the primary route to market for new UK offshore wind capacity. Contract length for fixed-bottom and floating offshore wind was extended from 15 to 20 years (CPI-indexed) starting with Allocation Round 7.

# Key Facts

- **Legal basis**: Energy Act 2013 (Electricity Market Reform).
- **Administrator**: Low Carbon Contracts Company (LCCC), a UK Government-owned company; counterparty to each CfD contract.
- **Auction runner (Delivery Body)**: National Energy System Operator (NESO), formerly National Grid ESO.
- **Policy owner**: Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ), formerly DECC/BEIS.
- **First allocation round (AR1)**: auction opened October 2014, results announced 19 February 2015; ~2.1 GW awarded, including the offshore wind projects East Anglia One (714 MW) and Neart na Gaoithe (448 MW); AR1 offshore wind strike price was £167/MWh.
- **Mechanism**: two-way contract for difference against a strike price, competitively set via sealed-bid pay-as-clear auctions within technology "pots."
- **Pots**: Pot 1 (established technologies), Pot 2 (emerging technologies, including floating offshore wind), and a dedicated offshore wind pot since AR4.
- **Cadence**: annual allocation rounds since 2022.
- **Contract length**: extended from 15 to 20 years, CPI-indexed, for offshore wind (fixed-bottom and floating) from AR7 onward.
