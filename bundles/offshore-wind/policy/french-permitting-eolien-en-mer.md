# Overview

Offshore wind projects in France go through a multi-stage administrative permitting process. It begins with a **débat public**, a public consultation organized by the Commission Nationale du Débat Public (CNDP), mandatory ahead of final siting decisions and involving public meetings and an online project dossier. Grid connection works led by RTE, covering both offshore and onshore cable segments, require a **Déclaration d'Utilité Publique (DUP)**.

Since a reform consolidating French environmental permitting (around 2017), offshore wind projects are covered by an **autorisation environnementale unique**: developers submit a single consolidated application to the État (via the water-police and classified-installations services), replacing what were previously several separate permits, alongside the concession d'utilisation du domaine public maritime (CUDPM).

The **loi APER** (loi n° 2023-175 of 10 March 2023, relative à l'accélération de la production d'énergies renouvelables) dedicates a title to accelerating offshore renewable energy: it requires each maritime façade to map priority zones for turbines and grid connections (by 2024, over a 10-year horizon), creates a single authorization regime covering construction, operation, and decommissioning, and routes all legal appeals against offshore wind farms and their grid connections directly to the Conseil d'État in first and last instance — an estimated two-year reduction in commissioning timelines. Historically, the full process from débat public to commissioning has averaged around ten years; loi APER's stated goal is to roughly halve this.

# Key Facts

- **Débat public**: mandatory public consultation led by CNDP ahead of final project siting.
- **DUP (Déclaration d'Utilité Publique)**: requested by RTE for grid connection infrastructure (offshore and onshore cable segments).
- **Autorisation environnementale unique**: single consolidated environmental authorization (reform ~2017), replacing multiple separate permits; delivered together with the CUDPM (maritime public domain use concession).
- **Loi APER** (n° 2023-175, 10 March 2023): dedicated title on offshore renewable energy acceleration — priority-zone mapping per maritime façade (2024, 10-year horizon), single authorization regime for construction/operation/decommissioning, and direct Conseil d'État jurisdiction (first and last instance) for legal appeals.
- **Timeline**: historically ~10 years average from débat public to commissioning; loi APER aims to cut this roughly in half.
- CRE's role concerns the tender/bidding process rather than permitting sequencing itself.
