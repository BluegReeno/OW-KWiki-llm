# Overview

The **Programmation Pluriannuelle de l'Énergie (PPE)** is France's central energy-planning instrument, established by the 2015 loi de transition énergétique pour la croissance verte and codified in the Code de l'énergie. Set by the ministry in charge of energy, it defines France's priorities and quantitative targets for energy production and consumption by technology, including offshore wind (éolien en mer), and sets the calendar for future offshore wind tenders (appels d'offres).

The PPE has gone through several iterations: **PPE1** (decree of 27 October 2016) and **PPE2** (decree of 21 April 2020), the latter covering two periods, 2019–2023 and 2024–2028. The successor, **PPE3**, covering 2026–2035, was presented on 12 February 2026 and its implementing decree was published on 13 February 2026, making it the current governing framework.

# Key Facts

- **Legal basis**: loi de transition énergétique pour la croissance verte (2015); codified in the Code de l'énergie.
- **PPE1**: adopted by decree, 27 October 2016.
- **PPE2**: adopted by decree, 21 April 2020; covers 2019–2023 and 2024–2028.
- **PPE2 offshore wind targets**: 2.4 GW installed capacity targeted for 2023; 5.2–6.2 GW (low/high option) targeted for 2028 (fixed-bottom and floating combined). The Commission de régulation de l'énergie (CRE) has indicated the 2028 target will not be met.
- **PPE3**: covers 2026–2035; presented 12 February 2026, decree published 13 February 2026.
- **PPE3 offshore wind targets**: 15 GW in service by 2035, with an ambition of 18 GW by 2037.
- **PPE3 tender pipeline**: a tenth offshore wind tender (AO10) is planned, covering multiple projects across several maritime façades for a combined 8–10 GW.
- **Employment**: PPE3 projects more than 120,000 additional jobs by 2030 linked to the plan overall, including around 28,000 in offshore wind specifically.
- A longer-horizon ambition of around 45 GW by 2050 was discussed in preparatory debates; this figure is not confirmed as formally adopted PPE3 text.
