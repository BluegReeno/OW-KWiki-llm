# Overview

The **EU Strategy to harness the potential of offshore renewable energy for a climate neutral future** was published by the European Commission on 19 November 2020. It sets EU-wide ambitions for offshore wind (fixed-bottom and floating) and other ocean energy technologies, and outlines the regulatory, planning, and investment measures needed to scale up deployment across EU maritime basins (North Sea, Baltic Sea, Atlantic, Mediterranean, Black Sea).

The strategy addresses maritime spatial planning, cross-border and coordinated grid infrastructure, reform of the regulatory and financial framework, mobilization of investment (including EU funding instruments), research and innovation, and supply chain development. It is complemented by regional political cooperation, most notably the **North Seas Energy Cooperation (NSEC)**, the EU high-level group coordinating offshore grid and wind buildout among North Sea countries, and by intergovernmental declarations such as the **Ostend Declaration**.

Since publication, EU Member States have informally revised ambitions upward: in January 2023 Member States indicated non-binding EU-wide goals of roughly 111 GW by 2030 and 317 GW by 2050, nearly double the original 2020 figures. The strategy also underpins broader EU renewable energy legislation, including RePowerEU and the revised Renewable Energy Directive (RED III).

# Key Facts

- **Published**: 19 November 2020, by the European Commission (DG ENER).
- **Original 2030 target**: at least 60 GW of offshore wind capacity installed EU-wide.
- **Original 2050 target**: at least 300 GW of offshore wind capacity EU-wide (plus 40 GW of ocean energy).
- **Baseline at publication**: approximately 12 GW installed offshore wind capacity in the EU.
- **Estimated investment need**: around €800 billion in offshore renewables infrastructure to 2050.
- **Revised (non-binding) EU-wide ambition (January 2023)**: ~111 GW by 2030 and ~317 GW by 2050.
- **Ostend Declaration** (North Sea Summit, Ostend, Belgium, 24 April 2023): nine countries (Belgium, Denmark, France, Germany, Ireland, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Norway, UK) committed to ~120 GW of North Sea offshore wind by 2030 and 300 GW by 2050.
- **North Seas Energy Cooperation (NSEC)**: coordination platform for offshore grid planning and joint wind buildout among North Sea Member States.
- Related legislation: RePowerEU plan and the revised Renewable Energy Directive (RED III) reinforce the renewable energy trajectory that offshore wind contributes to.
