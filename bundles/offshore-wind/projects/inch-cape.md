# Inch Cape Offshore Wind Farm

Inch Cape is a **1.1 GW** offshore wind farm under construction in the North
Sea, roughly 15 km off the Angus coast of Scotland. It is being developed by
**Inch Cape Offshore Limited (ICOL)**, a 50:50 joint venture between
**ESB** (Irish state-owned energy group) and **Red Rock Renewables**
(Edinburgh-based, part of China's SDIC Power group), formed in November 2020.

The wind farm will feature **72 Vestas V236-15.0 MW** turbines installed on
a combination of 54 monopile and 18 jacket foundations. Power is exported via
two 85 km export cables running from the offshore substation to a new onshore
substation at Cockenzie, East Lothian.

## Timeline

- **November 2020**: Red Rock Power and ESB form the ICOL joint venture.
- CfD support secured under [UK CfD Allocation Round 6](/tenders/uk-cfd-allocation-round-6.md)
  (a 266 MW top-up on top of an earlier AR4 award).
- **January 2025**: Financial close reached; project enters the offshore
  construction phase.
- **October 2025**: First of two 85 km export cables installed by Enshore
  Subsea using the cable-laying vessel (CLV) *CMOS Installer*.
- **June 2026**: All monopile foundations installed.
- **July 2026**: Second export cable installation campaign completed,
  installed by Enshore Subsea using cable supplied by Orient Cable (NBO);
  remaining interface works (offshore jointing, onshore/offshore platform
  jointing and termination) targeted for completion by end of 2026.
- **Late 2026**: First power expected.
- **2027**: Full commercial operation expected.
