# Overview

Dieppe-Le Tréport is an offshore wind farm under construction off the
Normandy coast of France, near Dieppe and Le Tréport (Seine-Maritime). The
Le Tréport zone was originally part of France's first tender round (AO1,
2011-2012) but was declared "sans suite" (without follow-up) in the 2012
award because its bid carried the highest electricity purchase price of
the five AO1 zones — see [France's first offshore wind tender
(AO1)](/tenders/french-ao1.md). The zone was re-tendered in France's second
round (AO2, 2014) and awarded to an Engie-led consortium; the project is
now associated with Ocean Winds (the EDP Renováveis/Engie offshore wind
joint venture) per offshoreWIND.biz's article categorization.

# Construction status

- **30 June 2026**: [DEME Offshore](/companies/deme-offshore.md) completed
  installation of all jacket foundations at the wind farm.
- Other companies associated with the project's foundation supply chain per
  offshoreWIND.biz reporting include Jan De Nul, Navantia Seanergies, and
  Windar Renovables.
