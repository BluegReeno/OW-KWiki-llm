# Overview

Saint-Brieuc is a 496 MW offshore wind farm in the Bay of Saint-Brieuc,
Brittany, roughly 16 km off the French coast. It is developed and
operated by Ailes Marines, which is now 100%-owned by
[Iberdrola](/companies/iberdrola.md) — Iberdrola held 70% from the
outset, with the remaining 30% held by Avel Vor (a joint venture of RES
and Caisse des Dépôts) until Iberdrola acquired full ownership in March
2020. The wind farm uses 62 [Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md)
8 MW turbines (SG 8.0-167 DD) and reached full commissioning on 28 May
2024, after a construction period considerably longer than the original
target.

Saint-Brieuc's development rights trace back to France's first offshore
wind tender round ([AO1](/tenders/french-ao1.md), launched 2011), with
Ailes Marines designated winner in April 2012 — one of the earliest French
offshore wind tender awards.
