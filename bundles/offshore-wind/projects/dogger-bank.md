# Overview

Dogger Bank Wind Farm is a offshore wind development in the North Sea, roughly
130–190 km off the Yorkshire / North East England coast, built in three
1.2 GW-class phases (A, B, C) totalling 3.6 GW — commonly cited as the
world's largest offshore wind farm. It is developed by a partnership of
SSE Renewables, [Equinor](/companies/equinor.md), and Vårgrønn (an
Eni Plenitude / HitecVision joint venture), each holding a stake in
Phases A–C. A further Phase D (roughly 1.5–2.0 GW) has cleared the UK's
Capacity Increase Programme but is not yet under construction and should
be treated as pipeline capacity, not built capacity.

Phase A reached first power in October 2023 and had all 95 turbines
installed by early 2026, with commercial operation targeted for
2025/2026. Phase B is under construction, with turbine installation
underway through 2026 and commissioning expected in 2027. Phase C has not
yet begun turbine installation as of the latest reporting. All three
phases use [GE Vernova](/companies/ge-vernova-offshore-wind.md)
Haliade-X turbines, and the project connects to shore via
[HVDC transmission](/technology/hvdc-grid-connection.md).

Note: Dogger Bank is a separate project from RWE's nearby Sofia offshore
wind farm, which sits in the same development zone but is unrelated.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capacity | 3.6 GW total (Phases A, B, C, ~1.2 GW each); Phase D (~1.5–2.0 GW) in pipeline, not yet under construction |
| Developer(s) | SSE Renewables (40%), [Equinor](/companies/equinor.md) (40%), Vårgrønn (20%) — Equinor operates the asset over its ~35-year life |
| Turbine supplier | [GE Vernova](/companies/ge-vernova-offshore-wind.md) Haliade-X — Phase A: 95 × 13 MW; Phase B: 95 × 13 MW; Phase C: 87 × 14 MW (277 turbines total across A–C) |
| Status | Phase A operational (first power Oct 2023); Phase B under construction (installation ongoing through 2026, commissioning ~2027); Phase C not yet installing turbines |
| Location | North Sea, ~130–190 km off the Yorkshire / North East England coast, UK |
| Foundation type | [Fixed-bottom](/technology/fixed-bottom-foundations.md) |
| Grid connection | [HVDC](/technology/hvdc-grid-connection.md) |
