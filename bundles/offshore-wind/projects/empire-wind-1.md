# Overview

Empire Wind 1 is an 810 MW offshore wind farm under construction in the
New York Bight, roughly 15–30 miles south of Long Island, with its
onshore hub at the South Brooklyn Marine Terminal. It is fully owned by
[Equinor](/companies/equinor.md), which took 100% ownership from a
former 50/50 arrangement with BP via a January 2024 asset swap (BP took
full ownership of Beacon Wind in exchange). The project uses 54
[Vestas](/companies/vestas.md) V236-15.0 MW turbines — Vestas' first
US offshore order. Empire Wind 1 was originally awarded in NYSERDA's
2019 solicitation and was re-awarded an offtake contract under New
York's fourth offshore wind solicitation (NY4) in February 2024.

The project has been affected by two federal stop-work orders under the
Trump administration: one issued and lifted in April–May 2025, and a
second, broader order affecting five US offshore wind projects issued
in December 2025. A federal judge granted Empire Wind a preliminary
injunction in January 2026, allowing construction to resume while
litigation over the underlying order continues. At the time of the
injunction the project was reported roughly 60% complete, with first
power expected in late 2026 and full commissioning in 2027.
