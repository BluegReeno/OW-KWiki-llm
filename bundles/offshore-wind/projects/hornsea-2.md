# Overview

Hornsea 2, located in the North Sea off the Yorkshire coast, was the
world's largest operational offshore wind farm when it reached full
commercial operation on 31 August 2022. It was developed and is operated
by [Ørsted](/companies/orsted.md), which retained full ownership until
October 2024, when Brookfield agreed to acquire a 12.45% minority stake
in a package deal covering four operational Ørsted UK wind farms
(Hornsea 1, Hornsea 2, Walney Extension, Burbo Bank Extension); the
transaction closed in December 2024, with Ørsted retaining majority
ownership (~87.55%) and operatorship, and holding a call option to
repurchase Brookfield's stake between years two and seven.

The wind farm uses 165 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW-class turbines
(SG 8.0-167 DD). Reported capacity figures vary slightly between
1,320 MW (commonly cited operational capacity) and 1,386 MW (a nameplate
figure derived from 165 × 8.4 MW) — this bundle uses the more commonly
cited 1,320 MW pending confirmation from Ørsted's own site.
