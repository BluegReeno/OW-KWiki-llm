# Overview

Vineyard Wind 1 is the first major commercial-scale offshore wind farm
built in the United States, located roughly 24 km (15 miles) south of
Martha's Vineyard, Massachusetts, with its onshore cable landing and
substation at Hyannis. It is a 50/50 joint venture between Avangrid
Renewables and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), and sells power
under agreements with National Grid, Eversource, and Unitil into the
ISO New England grid.

The project began delivering power in January 2024 and uses 62
[GE Vernova](/companies/ge-vernova-offshore-wind.md) Haliade-X 13 MW
turbines. In July 2024, a turbine blade fractured due to a manufacturing
defect at GE Vernova's Gaspé, Canada facility, and dozens of installed
blades were subsequently found to share the defect, halting parts of
operations and construction. As of March 2026, all 62 turbines were
reported installed and construction essentially complete, but as of
May 2026 roughly 36 of the 62 turbines remained offline pending
GE Vernova remediation work. Vineyard Wind and GE Vernova are in
ongoing litigation over the blade failure and associated costs, with a
Massachusetts court repeatedly ruling in Vineyard Wind's favor; full
resolution and full-fleet operation were not yet confirmed as of the
latest reporting.
