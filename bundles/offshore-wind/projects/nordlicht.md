# Overview

Nordlicht is a 1.6 GW offshore wind cluster being developed by
[Vattenfall](/companies/vattenfall.md) in the German North Sea, around
85 km north of Borkum. It comprises two wind farms, Nordlicht I (980 MW)
and Nordlicht II (630 MW), together totalling 112 Vestas V236-15.0 MW
turbines (68 at Nordlicht I, 44 at Nordlicht II). Once fully commissioned,
the cluster is expected to generate around 6 TWh/year. Vattenfall took
final investment decision on the project in January 2026, and both wind
farms are expected to enter operation in 2028.

# Construction status

- **1 July 2026**: [DEME Offshore](/companies/deme-offshore.md) installed
  the first monopile at the Nordlicht I site, marking the start of
  offshore construction on the cluster.
- Nordlicht I will comprise 68 monopile foundations and as many transition
  pieces (TPs); each monopile measures up to 80.5 m and weighs up to
  1,290 tonnes. Monopiles are manufactured by EEW Special Pipe
  Constructions (Rostock); transition pieces are supplied by CS Wind
  (Aalborg).
- DEME Offshore holds the transport & installation contract (awarded
  2024/2025) for monopile foundations and transition pieces at both
  Nordlicht I and Nordlicht II.
- Foundation installation at Nordlicht II is scheduled to start around a
  year after Nordlicht I's (i.e. roughly mid-2027).
- Vattenfall ran onshore transition-piece installation workflow trials
  earlier in 2026 to prepare for offshore construction.
