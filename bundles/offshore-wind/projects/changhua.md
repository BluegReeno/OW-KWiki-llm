# Overview

Ørsted's Greater Changhua offshore wind zone, located 35–60 km off the
coast of Changhua County, Taiwan, comprises four operating or
near-complete phases developed by [Ørsted](/companies/orsted.md) in
partnership with Taiwanese and Canadian investors: Greater Changhua 1
(605 MW) and Greater Changhua 2a (295 MW), commonly referred to together
as the 900 MW "GC1 & 2a" project, and Greater Changhua 2b (337 MW) and
Greater Changhua 4 (583 MW), referred to together as the 920 MW "GC2b &
4" project — for a combined 1,820 MW across the four phases.

GC1 and GC2a reached full commercial operation in April 2024, using
around 111–112 [Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md)
SG 8.0-167 DD 8 MW turbines. GC2b and GC4 use 66 Siemens Gamesa
SG 14-236 14 MW turbines — the first deployment of this turbine model
anywhere in the world — and had all turbines installed as of January
2026, with commercial operation expected in Q3 2026 after a delay caused
by a damaged export cable. Ørsted has progressively sold down equity in
each phase to Taiwanese investors (CDPQ/Cathay PE in GC1; Cathay Life
Insurance/Cathay Power as majority partner in GC2; Cathay Life in GC4)
while retaining a long-term operations and maintenance role from the
Port of Taichung.

# Key Facts

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capacity | 1,820 MW total: GC1 605 MW + GC2a 295 MW (900 MW combined, operational) + GC2b 337 MW + GC4 583 MW (920 MW combined, completing installation) |
| Developer(s) | [Ørsted](/companies/orsted.md) — GC1: 50% (CDPQ/Cathay PE 50%); GC2 (2a+2b): 45% (Cathay Life/Cathay Power 55%); GC4: 50% (Cathay Life 50%) |
| Turbine supplier | [Siemens Gamesa](/companies/siemens-gamesa.md) — GC1/2a: SG 8.0-167 DD, ~111–112 turbines, 8 MW each; GC2b/4: SG 14-236, 66 turbines, 14 MW each (first global deployment of this model) |
| Status | GC1 & 2a: fully operational since April 2024. GC2b & 4: all turbines installed Jan 2026, commercial operation expected Q3 2026 |
| Location | 35–60 km off Changhua County, Taiwan |
| Foundation type | [Fixed-bottom](/technology/fixed-bottom-foundations.md) (GC2b/4 uses piling-free suction bucket jacket foundations) |
