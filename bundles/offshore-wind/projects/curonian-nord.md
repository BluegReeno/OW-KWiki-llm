# Curonian Nord Offshore Wind Farm

Curonian Nord is a planned 700 MW offshore wind farm in the Lithuanian sector of the Baltic Sea,
Lithuania's first offshore wind project. The site sits around 37 kilometres off the Lithuanian
coast and covers an area of approximately 120 square kilometres.

Development rights were awarded in 2023 to a joint venture between
[Ignitis Renewables](/companies/ignitis-renewables.md) and Ocean Winds, via
[Lithuania's first offshore wind tender](/tenders/lithuania-first-offshore-wind-tender.md). In
2025, the partners signed a sale and purchase agreement under which Ignitis acquired full
ownership of the project.

In July 2026, the Environmental Protection Agency of Lithuania issued a positive Environmental
Impact Assessment (EIA) decision for the project, based on an EIA report assessing impacts on
marine ecosystems, biodiversity, landscape, fisheries and public health. The approval, subject to
mitigation and long-term monitoring measures, allows the project to proceed to its next
development stage. A construction permit is anticipated in 2027.
