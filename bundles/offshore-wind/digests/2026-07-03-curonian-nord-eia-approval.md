# 700 MW Curonian Nord Offshore Wind Project Gets Lithuanian EIA Green Light

Ignitis Renewables received a positive Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) decision from the
Environmental Protection Agency of Lithuania for its 700 MW [Curonian Nord](/projects/curonian-nord.md)
offshore wind farm, sited roughly 37 km off Lithuania's Baltic Sea coast. The EIA report assessed
impacts on marine ecosystems, biodiversity, landscape, fisheries, public health and other
factors; approval is conditional on mitigation measures and long-term environmental monitoring.

Curonian Nord is Lithuania's first offshore wind project, with development rights won by a joint
venture between [Ignitis Renewables](/companies/ignitis-renewables.md) and Ocean Winds in
[Lithuania's first offshore wind tender](/tenders/lithuania-first-offshore-wind-tender.md) (2023).
Ignitis acquired full ownership from Ocean Winds via a 2025 sale and purchase agreement. With the
EIA approval in place, the project moves to its remaining permitting stages, with a construction
permit anticipated in 2027.
